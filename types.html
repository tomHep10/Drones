Multirotor

These drones, equipped with multiple rotors, offer exceptional stability and are popular for photography, mapping, and inspections. They typically have shorter flight times due to energy-intensive hovering. Learn more about multirotor capabilities and uses at Grepow

Fixed-Wing

Fixed-Wing Drones: Known for their aerodynamics and efficiency, fixed-wing drones can cover large distances at high speeds, making them ideal for surveying and environmental monitoring. However, they need space for takeoff and landing. You can find more about their advantages at ScienceDirect

Single-Rotor

Single-Rotor Drones: Featuring one main rotor and a tail rotor, these drones are structurally similar to helicopters and are suitable for longer flights and carrying heavier loads. Their design makes them more efficient than multirotors. Discover more at Drone Tutorials.

Fixed-Wing Hybrid VTOL

Fixed-Wing Hybrid VTOL Drones: These drones combine fixed-wing and multirotor capabilities, allowing for vertical takeoff and landing along with extended flight distances. They're especially useful in difficult terrain where traditional takeoff isn't possible. Read more about hybrid VTOL drones at Hybrid VTOL Drones.

Tactical Rotor

Tactical Military Drones: Designed for reconnaissance, surveillance, and combat, these drones are highly specialized, capable of enduring challenging conditions and performing various military missions. Learn more at Military Drones.

Micro/Nano

Micro and Nano Drones: These miniature drones are ideal for indoor inspections, surveillance, and exploration in confined spaces. Despite their small size, they're packed with sophisticated technology. Find additional information at
